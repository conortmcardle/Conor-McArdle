About the Initiative

Project Manus is MIT's campus-wide effort to upgrade makerspaces, connect makers with resources through the Mobius web app, and foster student maker communities. The program touches more than 40 makerspaces and fabrication labs across MIT's campus and falls under the Morningside Academy for Design (MAD) and the Office of the Provost.

// my role

My Role

I was the sole communications professional for Project Manus — the in-house webmaster, filmmaker, photographer, graphic designer, audio editor, social media manager, and content strategist, often simultaneously. I built and maintained the website, produced video and print content, created newsletters and promotional assets, and told the story of a program that was redefining how making happens at a world-class university.

// key initiatives

Key Initiatives

  • Mobius — MIT's web app connecting students, faculty, and researchers with nearby maker resources, training, machine booking, and material purchases.
  • MakerLodge — The first-year undergraduate maker training program.
  • The Deep and Metropolis — Project Manus's two flagship community makerspaces, with student-mentor-based training.
  • The MET Makerspace — Communications and content support for the 17,000-square-foot state-of-the-art makerspace in the renovated Metropolitan Storage Warehouse, designed as the "gold standard" for next-generation university makerspaces.
  • Make Impact Consortium — An international collaboration with universities including the University of Derby (UK), Fontys University (Netherlands), and institutions in Jordan to build better makerspaces worldwide.
  • 3D Printing Service — Content and communications for MIT's campus-wide 3D printing service run in collaboration with Copytech.
