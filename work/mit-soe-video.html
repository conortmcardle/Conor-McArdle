Measuring the Speed of Light in the Infinite Corridor

Can you measure the speed of light down MIT's Infinite Corridor? Lee Zamir '95, MIT EECS alum and Technical Instructor at the MIT Edgerton Center, brings an MIT Open House classic from the 1950s back to life. Using a combination of vintage equipment from the archive of Harold "Doc" Edgerton, MIT Professor of Electrical Engineering, along with a laser and oscilloscope, Zamir sends a pulse of light down and back the third floor of the Infinite Corridor, directly measuring the travel time to calculate the speed of light.

For Zamir and J. Kim Vandiver, Professor of Mechanical Engineering and Director of the MIT Edgerton Center, the project is about more than measurement. It's about embodying the joyful curiosity that has long defined MIT's culture of hands-on experimentation — not only at the frontiers of the unknown, but with ideas that can still be experienced, tested, and shared.
